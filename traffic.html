Sanketa Technology Deep Dive

Edge AI traffic system for Bharat: adaptive, solar-powered, priority aware.

Challenge

Rapidly growing urban corridors and mixed vehicle classes overwhelm legacy fixed-timer signals—wasting time, fuel, and producing avoidable emissions. Lack of public countdown visibility fuels unpredictable driving behavior.

Objectives

  • Adaptive phase timing from live multi-sensor fusion.
  • Offline resilience & solar energy independence.
  • Priority routing for ambulances & high-capacity vehicles.
  • Open countdown feed for navigation & commuter transparency.
  • Sustainable reduction in idle emissions & noise.

Architecture Overview

  • Edge Controller: Rugged unit fusing camera + radar + legacy loop signals; holds models locally.
  • Sensing: Video for classification, radar for all-weather presence, loops for redundancy.
  • Streaming: Secure MQTT/gRPC uplink with redundancy.
  • Central (Optional): Aggregated analytics, remote updates & fleet-wide optimization experiments.
  • APIs: Internal optimization, public SPaT (Signal Phase & Timing), ETA, and metrics.
  • Storage: Time-series (phase + queues), object storage (clips), feature store (ML features).
  • Dashboard: Live map, queue heatmaps, overrides, audit logs.

AI & Optimization Strategy

We apply hybrid control: classical safety-constrained logic plus predictive and reinforcement learning components.

  • Arrival Prediction: Short-horizon temporal models (queue clearance & platoon arrival).
  • Phase optimization: Multi-agent RL in shadow mode until outperforming baseline algorithms (e.g., Webster/SCOOT/SCATS).
  • Safety layer: Deterministic constraints enforce min/max greens, pedestrian clearance, and emergency preemption.
  • Simulation loop: Offline SUMO replay with logged arrival profiles; drift detection triggers retraining.

Data Inputs

  • Real-time: Occupancy, approach speeds, queue lengths, turn counts, pedestrian actuations, weather, incidents.
  • Historical: Time-of-day patterns, seasonal indices, event calendars.
  • Derived: Arrival variability, platoon cohesion, spillback risk, pedestrian density.

Countdown Transparency

Controllers publish SPaT updates (100–500 ms). Platform normalizes and adds confidence scores; public endpoints provide current phase and remaining seconds. Consumers receive WebSocket streams or REST snapshots. Graceful fallback to static cycle estimates if adaptive prediction confidence is low.

Deployment Phases

  1. Pilot (5–10 intersections): instrumentation & baseline metrics.
  2. Shadow adaptive control off-peak; validation.
  3. Corridor rollout with coordinated green waves.
  4. Citywide expansion + public developer portal.
  5. Continuous optimization: incident prediction & eco-driving advisories.

Key Metrics

  • Average delay (sec/veh) & stop count.
  • Queue length percentiles & spillback events.
  • Travel time reliability (buffer index).
  • Estimated emissions reduction (CO₂, NOx).
  • Pedestrian wait and compliance.
  • API uptime & latency.
  • Safety proxies (hard braking, red-light violations).

Risks & Mitigations

  • Sensor failure: Health checks + multi-sensor redundancy.
  • Model drift: Scheduled evaluation + fallback logic.
  • Privacy: Edge anonymization, minimal retention, hashed probes.
  • Cybersecurity: Segmented network, mTLS, continuous pen testing.
  • Regulatory: Early authority engagement, transparent metrics.

Next Actions

  • Shadow optimization & performance comparison.
  • Public SPaT countdown API release.
  • Governance & data policy publication.
